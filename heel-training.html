I. The Marionette Drillexpand_more
1.Stand barefoot before a mirror, feet hip-width apart.
2.Imagine a string at the crown of your head pulling you upward.
3.Roll shoulders back and down — open the chest without arching the lower back.
4.Engage the core: draw the navel gently toward the spine.
5.Neutral spine — no swayback, no flat back. Tuck pelvis slightly.
6.Shift weight forward: 60–70% on the balls of your feet, 30–40% on heels.
7.Chin parallel to the floor. Gaze soft and slightly downward.
8.Breathe deeply. Hold 2 minutes. Repeat 5× daily.
II. Walking Techniqueexpand_more
1.Start: Feet in T or parallel, weight on the back foot.
2.Step: Heel contacts the ground first — then roll forward through the arch to the toe.
3.Stride: Short and precise — 10–12 inches maximum. Longer equals instability.
4.Line: One foot directly in front of the other. This creates the hip sway.
5.Arms: Swing naturally from the shoulder, not the elbow. Head level, no bobbing.
6.Speed: Slow and deliberate. Confidence comes with practice.
7.Turns: Pivot on the ball of the foot. Keep the upper body still.
III. Daily Foot Careexpand_more

Perform this 10–15 min ritual after every training session.

→Soak in warm water with Epsom salts or lavender oil for 5–10 minutes after removal.
→Pat dry and moisturize generously — urea cream is excellent for calluses.
→Ankle circles — 10 each direction, each foot.
→Calf stretch against wall — 30 seconds × 3 per leg.
→Toe spreads — separate toes gently with fingers, hold 20 seconds.
→Plantar fascia — roll a frozen water bottle under the arch.
→Self-massage: knead arches, balls of feet, and heels with thumbs.
IV. Troubleshootingexpand_more
"I keep wobbling and losing balance."
Fix: Slow down. Shorten stride. Engage core. Practice the single-line drill. Check shoe fit — too loose means slipping.
"My calves and Achilles are aching."
Fix: Stretch more frequently. Reduce height temporarily. Ice after sessions. Build calf strength with seated raises in flats first.
"I have lower back pain after sessions."
Fix: Check pelvic position — likely over-arching. Strengthen core with planks. Ensure weight is not pitched too far forward.
"Blisters and hot spots."
Fix: Break in heels gradually. Use blister tape or gel pads. Ensure proper sizing. Moisture-wicking socks or foot powder.
"My knees knock or lock."
Fix: Maintain a micro-bend in the knees. Turn feet out slightly (5–10°). Lead with the ball rather than striking the heel hard.
V. When to Stop — Tell Sirexpand_more
🛑 Stop Immediately If:
Pain persisting more than 48 hours · Swelling, bruising, or numbness · Inability to bear weight normally · Blisters that break or show signs of infection

Caring for the body that serves is itself an act of submission. Do not push through real injury.
