Posture — The Marionette Drill
  1. 1.Stand barefoot before a mirror, feet hip-width apart.
  2. 2.Imagine a string at the crown of your head pulling you upward.
  3. 3.Roll shoulders back and down — open the chest without arching the lower back.
  4. 4.Engage the core: draw the navel gently toward the spine.
  5. 5.Neutral spine — no swayback, no flat back. Tuck pelvis slightly.
  6. 6.Shift weight forward: 60–70% on the balls of your feet, 30–40% on heels.
  7. 7.Chin parallel to the floor. Gaze soft and slightly downward.
  8. 8.Breathe deeply. Hold 2 minutes. Repeat 5× daily.
Walking Technique
  1. 1.Start: Feet in T or parallel, weight on the back foot.
  2. 2.Step: Heel contacts the ground first — then roll forward through the arch to the toe.
  3. 3.Stride: Short and precise — 10–12 inches maximum. Longer equals instability.
  4. 4.Line: One foot directly in front of the other. This creates the hip sway.
  5. 5.Arms: Swing naturally from the shoulder, not the elbow. Head level, no bobbing.
  6. 6.Speed: Slow and deliberate. Confidence comes with practice.
  7. 7.Turns: Pivot on the ball of the foot. Keep the upper body still.
Daily Foot Care (10–15 min)
  • →Soak in warm water with Epsom salts or lavender oil for 5–10 minutes after removal.
  • →Pat dry and moisturize generously — urea cream is excellent for calluses.
  • →Ankle circles — 10 each direction, each foot.
  • →Calf stretch against wall — 30 seconds × 3 per leg.
  • →Toe spreads — separate toes gently with fingers, hold 20 seconds.
  • →Plantar fascia — roll a frozen water bottle under the arch.
  • →Self-massage: knead arches, balls of feet, and heels with thumbs.
Troubleshooting
Wobbling / Loss of Balance
Slow down. Shorten stride. Engage core. Practice the single-line drill. Check shoe fit — too loose means slipping.
Calf / Achilles Pain
Stretch more frequently. Reduce height temporarily. Ice after sessions. Build calf strength with seated raises in flats first.
Lower Back Ache
Check pelvic position — likely over-arching. Strengthen core with planks (off heels). Ensure weight is not pitched too far forward.
Blisters or Hot Spots
Break in heels gradually. Use blister tape or gel pads. Ensure proper sizing. Moisture-wicking socks or foot powder.
Knees Knocking or Locking
Maintain a micro-bend in the knees. Turn feet out slightly (5–10°). Focus on leading with the ball rather than striking the heel hard.
When to Stop — Inform Sir Immediately
  • ∴Pain persisting more than 48 hours after training
  • ∴Swelling, bruising, or numbness
  • ∴Inability to bear weight normally
  • ∴Blisters that break or show signs of infection

Caring for the body that serves is itself an act of submission. Do not push through real injury.
