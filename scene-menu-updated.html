How it works
Six steps. One map.
Move through Headspace → Intent → Canvas → Scene → Seal → Review. Each step is a layer — how you're feeling, what you want, how you want to serve.

How This Works
The protocol explained, in your words
The Six Steps
1
Headspace
Before anything else, you check in with yourself. How are you arriving tonight — emotionally, physically, mentally? What do you need? What are you afraid of? Sir reads this first. It sets the tone for everything that follows.
2
Intent
Here you tell Sir what you're bringing to the scene — your role tonight, the vibe you're craving, how you want to be handled, what you want him to hear from you, and where you want your mind to go. This is your desire, made explicit.
3
Canvas
The physical layer. What you'll wear, where you want his attention on your body, and what sensory experiences you're open to tonight. You're painting the scene before it begins.
4
Scene
The deeper negotiation. Bondage, toys, impact play — each with its own intensity and style. This is where you show Sir what you're ready for and how you want it delivered.
5
Seal
How you want to serve Sir. The acts of service you're offering, how Sir finishes, and what you need afterward. Aftercare is not an afterthought — it lives here alongside everything else.
6
Review
The complete map. Sir sees every selection in one place. He can add his own notes before the scene. You can save it, copy it, or share it with him directly. This is the handover.
Special Features
gavel
Sir's Directive
Sir takes the controls. The entire app flips to his perspective — he builds the map for you, choosing everything himself. You receive your instructions at the end.
casino
Surprise Me
One tap and the app builds a complete scene at random — every section filled. Good for when you want to surrender the choice entirely and just see what comes.
history
Past Scenes
Every saved map is kept here. You can revisit, compare, and after a scene — check in. Rate how it felt. Answer a few questions. Let the record reflect what actually happened, not just what you planned.
insights
Scene Insights
Over time, patterns emerge. What you ask for most. When you're most active. Moments that mattered, pulled from your check-ins. A quiet record of your dynamic, growing.
casino
The Dice
Every section has a small dice button. Tap it and one item from that section is selected at random. For when you know you want something in this category, but want to let chance decide what.
block
Hard Limits
Set your hard limits in Settings. If anything you've selected matches a limit, it's flagged with a warning on the Review screen so Sir sees it immediately. Your boundaries travel with the map.
Navigating the App
linear_scale
The Progress Bar
The six gold segments at the top aren't just decoration — tap any segment to jump directly to that step. You don't have to move through them in order. Go back, skip ahead, revisit anything.
touch_app
Chips & Pills
Tap once to select. Tap again to deselect. Tap a selected chip a second time to reveal its description — what it means, what it signals to Sir. Most chips have one hidden.
casino
The Dice Button
Every section title has a small 🎲 dice icon. Tap it and one item from that section is randomly selected and its description shown. Good for when you're unsure, or want to be surprised within a category.
skip_next
Skip Reflections
On the Headspace step, the journal questions are optional. A Skip reflections → link sits at the bottom — tap it to move straight to Intent without filling anything in.
edit_note
Sir's Notes
On the Review screen, there's a collapsible Sir's Notes field. Sir opens it, adds his modifications or intentions, and those notes travel with the map when it's saved or shared.
bookmark
Save vs Share
Save This Scene stores the map in Past Scenes so you can revisit and check in after. Share with Sir (the gold button at the bottom) sends the full formatted map as text — ready to copy or send directly.
restart_alt
Reset
The reset icon in the top right clears all selections and starts a fresh map. It does not delete saved scenes — only the current unsaved session is cleared.
