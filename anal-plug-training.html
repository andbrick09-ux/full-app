Philosophy & Objective

Safety & Essentials

Lubrication Is Non-Negotiable
Always use high-quality, thick anal lubricant. The rectum does not self-lubricate. Reapply during extended wear. Water-based for silicone plugs; silicone-based for metal/glass plugs.

Clean plugs before and after every use with antibacterial toy cleaner or hot soapy water. Never go from anal to vaginal without thorough cleaning. Consider a light anal douche before extended sessions.

Duration Limits
Beginners: max 30 min first sessions. Never exceed 4 hours continuous wear. Remove immediately if you feel sharp pain (dull pressure is normal), numbness, or bleeding.

Flared Base Only
ONLY use plugs with a flared base or T-bar. The rectum creates suction — a plug without a proper base can become irretrievable and require medical attention.
🛑
Stop Signals
Sharp/stabbing pain, bleeding, persistent discomfort after removal, or inability to remove the plug. Any of these = stop immediately. Pain is NOT part of training — pressure and stretch are; pain is not.

Material Guide
Silicone: Best for beginners. Flexible, body-safe, comfortable. Stainless steel: Smooth insertion, temperature play. Glass: Beautiful, easy to clean, temperature play. Avoid jelly/rubber/PVC — they are porous and unsafe.

"I can't relax enough for insertion."

"The plug keeps sliding out."

"It's comfortable at first but becomes painful after 20 minutes."

"I feel like I need to use the bathroom constantly."

"I'm stuck between sizes — current is easy, next is too much."
